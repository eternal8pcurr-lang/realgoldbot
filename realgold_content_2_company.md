1. THE FUNDING ROUND (the current raise) — including "Keep-ons"

What is being raised right now: a promissory note

RealGold's current, present-day fundraising vehicle is a simple promissory note — not a token sale, and not yet a "Keepon." The note is offered privately to a small number of qualified investors in RealGold's network as a transitional funding structure.

Key note terms (from the term sheet and promissory note):

Borrower: Regenerative Assets LLC (a Wyoming software company that builds the RealGold infrastructure).

Launch round size: up to $1,500,000.

Interest rate: 12% per annum, simple interest.

Maturity: 5 years from the date of issuance.

Interest payment: accrues annually, payable on the maturity date unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Repayment: in U.S. Dollars, or — at the lender's option — by other means of equivalent value.

Prepayment: the borrower may prepay in whole or part at any time, without penalty.

Conversion to equity: on a default, purchase, or liquidation event, the outstanding principal and accrued interest may, at the lender's option, convert into equity in Regenerative Assets LLC, on valuation and terms determined at the time of conversion.

Status: not a registered offering; offered only to qualified/accredited investors; the note has not been registered under the Securities Act of 1933.

What "Keep-ons" (Keepons) actually are

A "Keepon" is a planned future offering — described as a time-discounted right to acquire RealGold tokens (or related assets). Keepons do not exist as a defined, available instrument today.

The connection to the note: if Regenerative Assets later conducts a "Keepon Offering" during the term of the note, the borrower intends, but is not obligated, to (a) offer the noteholder the chance to participate on preferential early-investor terms, and (b) allow the noteholder to apply some or all of their outstanding principal and interest as consideration in that Keepon Offering.

Critical framing the Assistant must preserve: the Keepon roll-over is an expression of present intent only — it is NOT a binding obligation to conduct a Keepon Offering, to issue any instrument, or to accept any exchange, unless and until set out in separate executed definitive agreements. The Assistant must describe Keepons as a forthcoming, intended, non-binding future opportunity, never as a promised or guaranteed instrument, and route specifics (terms, timing, discount) to the team.

Why the funding is structured this way

RealGold intends that RealGold itself NOT be classified by the SEC as a security — because it represents a perpetual fee interest in real estate, not a promise of future yields or the efforts of others. To stay conservative about offering tokens, RealGold will not offer a token to investors until there is actual present-day gold backing it. The promissory note is the transitional bridge until gold assets are in the system. The shared (non-binding) intention is that the note may be repaid early via a gold-backed instrument once gold assets exist.

4. THE DUAL-MARKET MODEL (real estate + token)

RealGold's distinguishing feature is offering gold-in-ground in two markets: as fractional real estate (the Delaware Statutory Trust) and as a gold-backed crypto token.

Why real property: gold affixed to and not currently separable from land is legally real property, not personal property — legally, in-ground gold IS real estate. RealGold structures a real-estate offering that behaves like gold (tracking gold rather than real-estate prices), which is novel value for investors with significant real-estate portfolios.

How it behaves like gold: the gold is unitized as real estate via a Delaware Statutory Trust (preserving Section 1031 eligibility), with operations structured to eliminate operating costs/profits so it behaves like passive ownership of gold. A transfer protocol relates the price of a Delaware Statutory Trust interest to a pro-rated portion of the registered ounces.

The token is a separate, gold-backed instrument issued by the RealGold Treasury, intended to be redeemable for physical gold via a bullion partner.

5. RESOURCE UNITIZATION METHODOLOGY

All resources are first confirmed by an independent N.I. 43-101–compliant geological report by a Qualified Person, before registration in the Real Assets Registry.

Confidence-based valuation ratios are applied to total Assayed Resources to determine what is eligible for registration, reflecting the three industry classifications: Measured (high confidence), Indicated (moderate), Inferred (lower confidence).

The progression is: Assayed Resources (in the certified report) → Registered Resources (portion eligible for registration) → Releasable Resources (portion eligible for market release).

RealGold releases to market a range of 20% up to a maximum of 50% of total Unitizable Resources, time-released over 20–40 years — creating stable supply growth and a steady stream of capital for stewardship, and making the asset over-collateralized.

(For the Courbet-specific holdback and the current conveyance ratios — ~10% held at mine, then 3:1 / 4:1 / 5:1 by estates committed — see the holdback knowledge; that is the current model for Courbet.)

6. THE TRIPARTITE ESTATE FRAMEWORK

RealGold rebundles unified land-ownership rights into three interconnected, mutually accountable estates, each a guardian of a vital aspect of the land:

Mineral Estate — holds rights to the minerals beneath the surface, creating a financial foundation without extraction.

Biological Estate — supports living-systems integrity; ensures decisions enhance biodiversity and ecological health.

Social Estate — empowers the commons with economic engines for local prosperity and broad value creation.

It initializes with a Declaration of Tripartite Estates, legally rebundling ownership into three freehold, deedable estates (entity type varies by jurisdiction). A structured release schedule grants asset access to titleholders who commit to and meet regenerative performance metrics. When value is created through the Mineral Estate, it flows to enhance the Biological and Social estates — turning the traditional profit-vs-preservation tension into mutual accountability. The structure is also designed to be very difficult for a hostile actor to unwind for extractive purposes.

7. THE CHINESE WALL: EVALUATION vs. ACQUISITION

This is RealGold's current posture on how the certification side and the token side relate (and it replaces any "guaranteed buyer" framing):

Real Assets Foundation independently evaluates projects through a series of ongoing assessments across mineral, biological, and social conservation dimensions. The evaluation is ongoing and can change over time. The Foundation may issue a certification mark signaling assayed quality for the RealGold system. The Foundation produces a quality signal — it does not set a price.

The RealGold Treasury (token side) independently decides what assets to acquire and at what price, drawing on many inputs — current markets, comparable assets, other in-ground gold/crypto projects — among which the Foundation's evaluation is one signal. Pricing and the decision to buy are formed independently on the acquisition side.

The body that certifies is walled off from the body that prices and acquires — a "Chinese wall." This is what keeps certification independent from pricing.
